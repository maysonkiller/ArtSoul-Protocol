ArtSoul Auction Protocol

A discovery-first auction model where the artwork comes first and the NFT is created only after real collector settlement.

Why ArtSoul Is Different

ArtSoul is not a generic mint page or an OpenSea clone. It is an auction-first art protocol built around commitment, discovery, and creator protection.

  • Artwork can be discovered before it becomes an NFT.
  • Bids use deposits, reducing spam without forcing every bidder to lock the full bid.
  • NFTs are minted only after successful settlement.
  • Resale floors are created only by completed payment, not by abandoned bids.
  • Creators keep attribution and resale royalties.

The goal is a cleaner art market: fewer empty tokens, stronger collector signals, and a visible path from discovery to ownership.

Lifecycle Overview

1. Artwork

The creator publishes artwork metadata and media. At this stage, the artwork is visible and discoverable, but no NFT has been minted.

  • The creator remains permanently attributed.
  • The artwork can receive discovery signals such as Like, Would Buy, and Watching.
  • The artwork can later move into an auction.

2. Auction

The creator launches an auction with a starting price and one of three supported durations: 24 hours, 36 hours, or 48 hours.

  • Supported testnet path: Base Sepolia and Ethereum Sepolia.
  • Creators cannot bid on their own artwork.
  • Bidders cannot outbid themselves.
  • Late bids can extend the auction to reduce last-second sniping.

3. Settlement

When the auction ends, the highest bidder becomes the settlement candidate and receives a 24 hour window to complete payment.

  • The winner pays the remaining balance.
  • The deposit counts toward the final price.
  • The NFT is still not minted until settlement succeeds.

4. NFT Birth

If settlement succeeds, the NFT is minted and transferred to the winner. This is the moment the artwork becomes an on-chain collectible.

  • The final settled price becomes the protected resale floor.
  • The creator receives sale proceeds after the platform fee.
  • The creator royalty is preserved for future resale.

5. Resale

After minting, the owner can list the NFT for resale. The resale price must be at or above the protected floor created by successful settlement.

Deposit-Backed Bidding

ArtSoul uses deposits to make bids meaningful without forcing every bidder to pay the full bid upfront.

Required Deposit

required deposit = max(10% of bid, 0.01 ETH)

This keeps small bids from becoming spam while keeping serious auctions accessible.

Next Bid Rule

next bid = max(highest bid + 0.01 ETH, highest bid + 2.5%)

The absolute increment keeps small auctions moving. The percentage increment keeps larger auctions serious.

Outbid Collectors

Previous bidders do not lose their deposits when they are outbid. Their deposits move into a withdrawal balance and can be claimed safely through the contract.

Winner Commitment

The winner's deposit is applied to settlement if they complete payment. If they fail to settle within 24 hours, the locked deposit is split between the artist and the platform.

Anti-Sniping

ArtSoul includes auction extension rules to reduce last-second sniping and give serious collectors time to respond.

  • Trigger window: final 10 minutes.
  • Extension length: 10 minutes.
  • Maximum total extension: 60 minutes.

The goal is not to make auctions endless. The goal is to avoid unfair last-second endings while keeping a clear cap on auction duration.

Successful Settlement

Settlement is the point where market interest becomes ownership. Until this step succeeds, there is no NFT and no resale floor.

Final bid: 2.0 ETH
Deposit already paid: 0.2 ETH
Settlement payment: 1.8 ETH
Result: NFT minted to winner
Protected resale floor: 2.0 ETH
  • Platform fee: 2.5%.
  • Creator royalty on resale: 7.5%.
  • The original creator is the royalty receiver.
  • The NFT is minted only after payment completes.

Failed Settlement

If the winner does not complete payment during the 24 hour settlement window, the artwork does not become an NFT.

  • No NFT is minted.
  • No protected resale floor is created.
  • The locked winner deposit is split 80% to the artist and 20% to the platform.
  • The artwork can be relaunched or handled through a future supported sale path.

This protects creators from unserious winning bids while avoiding artificial floors based on unpaid prices.

Resale and Royalties

After a successful settlement, the owner can resell the NFT. The resale price must respect the protected floor created by the completed auction price.

  • Resale below the protected floor is rejected.
  • The creator receives a 7.5% royalty on resale.
  • The platform receives a 2.5% fee on completed sales.
  • Floor protection applies only after successful settlement.

Discovery and Trust

ArtSoul discovery is designed to surface meaningful attention, not just the loudest raw metrics.

  • Likes show broad appreciation.
  • Would Buy signals stronger collector intent.
  • Watching tracks ongoing interest.
  • Successful settlement is the strongest trust signal.
  • Low-trust signals can be weighted lower without banning users automatically.

Discovery signals do not affect settlement, floor price, royalties, mint rights, or winner selection.

Genesis and Collections

Genesis status is reserved for the first 100 eligible participants who show real ecosystem activity. Eligibility is based on meaningful participation such as profile creation, artwork publishing, auction participation, successful settlement, and artwork interactions.

Collections and drops are separated from normal user artwork feeds. They are intended for platform-approved launches, collaborations, and Genesis-style drops, while the gallery remains the main discovery hub for user artworks.

FAQ

Can I explore without connecting a wallet?

Yes. Guest exploration is part of the product. Wallet connection is required for bidding, settlement, buying, selling, listing, profile edits, publishing artwork, and other on-chain or account-bound actions.

Is the NFT minted when the artist publishes?

No. Publishing creates a discoverable artwork. The NFT is minted only after successful auction settlement.

Does the highest bid immediately create the floor?

No. The protected floor is created only when the winner completes settlement.

What happens if the winner does not settle?

The NFT is not minted, no floor is created, and the locked deposit is split 80% to the artist and 20% to the platform.

Can a creator bid on their own artwork?

No. Creator self-bidding is blocked.

What networks are prepared?

Base Sepolia and Ethereum Sepolia are the current testnet path. Rialo remains a future target.
